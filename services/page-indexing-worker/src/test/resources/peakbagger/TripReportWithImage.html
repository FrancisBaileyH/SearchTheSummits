    Motorized Transport to Trailhead:Car
Peak:Mount Baker
    Location:USA-Washington
    Elevation:3286 m / 10781 ft

Ascent Trip Report

Went up as part of the Alpinism 1 course with American Alpine Institute. We were in a group of 9 which included our 2 guides/instructors. Hiked to Sandy Camp on Monday the 22nd. Pitched our tents in one of the many cleared sites where rock walls had been built over the years for protection from the wind. After 3 days of courses, we woke up at midnight for the long day ahead of us. Each guide led a rope team. I was in the back of the lead team. This was my first time really on a wet glacier and walking across the first few crevasses at 3am was certainly an experience. The light of my headlamp disappearing into the abyss was eerie yet galvanizing. I was using the skills I had learned over the week, and to know that I was being trusted as the team's anchor really helped my confidence. I was so focused on keeping a steady pace I almost didn't notice how incredible the view was as the sun came up. After an exhausting and hair-raising push in the half mile leading to the Sherman crater, we took a break. In the last hour, we pushed the Roman Wall and made the summit at 9am. If anyone finds the note labeled "Hell yeah, brother," that was me. I learned so much on the trip, I could spend all day writing about it. In the end, I feel ready to head back to Alaska and tackle some more serious and technical peak. Mountaineering has officially captured me.

I also had a huge crush on my guide. Tammy if you read this, I could listen to that Kiwi accent all day.
