Ascent Trip Report

Little Hector is not so little.
This is a peak that tops out at 3100m, with an elevation gain of 1260m. It features a diverse array of terrain; my partner and I followed the Kane guidebook route for the upper parts but chose to add an extra adventure by scrambling up and across slabs on the first ridge. Thus the ascent could be roughly divided into four parts: approach and headwall of Hector Creek; hike through the upper valley, with an ascent of the slabs; scramble of the "pyramid" shape scree and rock slope; and the final slope towards the summit. The upper third of this peak happened to be snowy...not much but enough to render most surface slick, adding some further challenge in terms of footing.
On the descent, we chose a different line, shuffling down a prominent drainage and eventually open slopes south of our original ascent line - se we didn't go back to the Hector Creek gulley or headwall. This way proved much quicker but was not without some problem-solving.

I would say that Little Hector is an amazing day out and it likely constituted a highlight of my 2021 season. But is a big peak, exposed to the weather - and it was cold, windy and miserable in the uppermost reaches. One could easily experience two, three seasons in one day up there.

Summit views are some of the best I have seen in CDN Rockies. The wild and craggy, dry front ranges of Banff are on display to the east and the glaciated ranges to the west stand in contrast. Nearby Mt. Hector, separated by a ridge and a glacier, adds the snow and ice to the palette of mountain colours.
