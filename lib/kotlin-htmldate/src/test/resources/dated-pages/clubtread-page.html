Watersprite Lake 2022-09-25
I've been a lurker here for a while but finally here is my first post. Also it's a month late but better than never. I had learnt about Watersprite Lake quite some time ago and had it on my bucket list. Finally with the extended summer we were having I coordinated with a few friends and family set a date. A few friends dropped out but my wife, youngest daughter and I left home about 7:00 am and arrived at Mamquam road turn off in Squamish about 8:30. This was my first time here and I had reviewed the directions and google earth map but I'll add a little bit of clarification. From hwy 99 follow Mamquam fsr staying left at 3.7 km. At 12.7 km stay left (this is still Mamquam fsr) and cross the bridge across Mamquam river, then at 12.9 km cross the bridge over Skookum creek and about 100m later turn left between the signs for Skookum hydro. This is Skookum fsr and from here on the road gets rougher. We saw a Porsche Cayenne, Subarus and etc but you will want decent clearance if you value your cars undercarriage and good tires. At 13.9 km stay left, at 15.9 km stay right ( the left is a rougher option that rejoins later), at 15.9 km stay left at a candy cane shape vent (right, I believe, takes you to the winter route), at 16.4 stay right at another candy cane vent (left rejoins 15.9 km), right again at 19.1 km and arrive the parking area about 19.5 km. Allow 1 hour driving from hwy 99. There is quite a nice outhouse here (as far as outhouses go). Just at the start of the trail you pass the inlet for the Skookum Creek hydro project. The trail follows old logging roads most of the way and is well marked except for a few spots on the talus slopes. Along the way we got good views of the Elfin Lakes / Diamond Head area and the Tantalus and Tetrahedron areas and the Sky Pilot area. We took about 3 1/2 hours to hike 9.2 km, according to my gps, and about the same down. Several of us had a quick swim in the chilly lake.
